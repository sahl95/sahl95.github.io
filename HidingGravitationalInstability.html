Hiding Signatures of Gravitational Instability in Protoplanetary Discs

Abstract

We carry out three dimensional SPH simulations to show that a migrating giant planet strongly suppresses the spiral structure in self-gravitating discs. We present mock continuum observations which show that a disc with a 3MJup planet appears completely axisymmetric with short integration times (< 1 hour), while with longer integration times, spiral arms from the planet become detectable. Our modelling of the gas kinematics shows that the planet could be detected using optically thin 13C16O. Our results show that it is not necessary to limit the gas mass of discs by assuming high dust-gas mass ratios in order to explain a lack of spiral features that would otherwise be expected in high mass discs.
